SOCIETY DAY - DECEMBER 2003

This year being the 50th anniversary of the Lokmanya Mandal, there was a lot of expectation amongst us all that the society day was going to be a special event. And one must say that the entire event did live up to everyone’s expectations! In fact, this year’s society day must rank amongst the best in recent times. The ‘society day fever’ had reached its peak when the last of the sports event ‘ treasure hunt’ was held on 14th! One could feel the excitement that the society day was about to begin in a few days. The buzz was definitely around the corner that this year’s day was going to be awesome

The society day began with the ‘food fair’ on 21st evening. They were some nice stalls of dosas, bhel, pattice, chinese, custard and all. So, all of us could enjoy the gastronomic delights. But, the ‘fun’ part began after the food fair. We had impromptu games conducted by Devendra, Mrs. Sucheta Lele, Mrs. Medha Dandekar etc in which all including kakas and kakus participated. These were held in the front of the Lokmanya Tilak statue and all of us made a lot of noise playing them. I think everyone who was watching them from their balconies were enjoying the games too. We then had ‘antakshari’ at the same venue with mikes and all! This year was a record with 28 participants amongst them. It started off with a compulsory round with everyone in the team having to sing! All in all, the society day had begun with a bang!

This year also saw a number of ‘ mahervashnis’ and their children participating in the day with Ketki Vaidya, Ashwini Limaye , Kalyani Katdare and others with their children. On 23rd night, there was the ‘pataka’ session in which everyone right from ketki’s 2 year old daughter to men of 40 years old participated! We had awesome vada pavs made by the society day contractor Mrs. Yamini Vaidya and good coffee too. This time, the ‘workers’ being many, we finished the work by 4 a.m. 24th December night started off with a fancy dress competition followed by an orchestra organized by ashwin joglekar and gang. Rajesh and sonali vartak from the society also participated in the same and one was treated to some really good hindi and marathi songs. Ashwin and rajesh were in full flow!

25th December was the Lokmanya Mandal day, it being its golden jubilee! We had Dr. Sane, principal of Ruia college who gave a decent speech.. At night, there were some fabulous programmes in which everyone from 2 year old kids to the elders performed. Andy Rahalkar and gang had organized this fantastic record dance of 6 two yr old kids and they were simply fabulous and got a lot of cheers. Then, we had some record dances by some of the girls in the society such as Kalika and Dipti Sathe, Shruti Vaidya and Swapnapriya Ranade, choreographed by Radhika Agarkar and they were very good. We also had two one act plays performed by the young brigade [ age group 6-16]! They were ‘ VAYAM MOTHAM KHOTAM’ written by the legendary pula deshpande and ‘VITHAL TO ALA RE ALA!’. All of the performers were really good.

26th December evening was Lokmanya Bhagini Mandal day and the chief guest in the evening was Ms. Cecilia Carvalo who is a writer and a social activist. She spoke really well which was appreciated by all. In the night, we had a three act play ‘ ACHAT GAVCHI AFAT MAVSHI’ written by Ratnakar Matkari and directed by our very own Satish Joshi. The play was fabulous and all the young turks performed exceedingly well. Madhur Joshi played the central character and was pretty good. Everyone had a great time sitting down and cheering the young ones who were themselves having a pretty good time on the stage.

The chief guest of the main day on 27th was Mr. Vijay Kuvlekar, editor of ‘Sakal’ newspaper. His speech was simply brilliant and everyone just loved it. He spoke about the practical situation prevalent in India today and painted a very realistic picture through many examples. At night was the piece de resistance. It was the turn of the famous play ‘TI PHULRANI’ written by once again the legendary Pula and directed by Ajay Lele. One must say Ajay Lele did a pretty good job and his team performed well. Apurva Oak who played the role of Phulrani was really good and was appreciated by a lot by the audience. So were Shrirang Mulye, Yeshwant (Navin) Joshi, Mrs. Anuradha Kane, Chaya Katdare, Namrata kale, Sachin Shende, Alok Dongre, Andy Rahalkar, Mrs. Manisha Vartak and Amol Upadhye.. All the characters performed well and deserved all the applause. There is a VCD of the play which will come out soon. All in all, a good effort by all of them.

28th morning was sahbhojan and as usual was preceded by the young guns playing cricket before that! The menu for lunch was matarachi usal, puri, shrikhand, pulao, sol kadhi etc. and the contractor for the same was Mr. Vikas Dongre. The food was overall good. All in all, thus ended the society day which left us craving for more next year!
