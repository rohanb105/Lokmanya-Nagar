Dassera


Also known as "Dusshera" amongst the North Indians, this Hindu festival is celebrated to recognise the victory of Lord Ram over the demon Ravan i.e. the triumph of truth over evil. In many places all over India, it is usually celebrated by burning a huge effigy of Ravan specially created for the said purpose. This festival usually takes place in October, about a fortnight before Diwali. In our society, there is a special "puja" conducted under the auspices of the Lokmanya Mandal in the evening outside the office of the society. The significance of this puja is that it is usually performed by a young resident couple of the society who is newly married in the same or earlier year. This puja is called "Shamipujan" since it involves the leaves of the "Shami" tree. In fact, in our Hindu mythology, great significance is attached to this tree and on this day, the leaves of this tree are distributed as gold by everyone at home and outside. After the puja, everyone present are treated with sweets and coffee.
