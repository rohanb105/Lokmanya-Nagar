Diwali


Also known as the "festival of lights", this mother of all festivals is usually celebrated around late October or November. There is an atmosphere of joy, exuberance and enthusiasm all over. Everyone waits for this festival for days and there is a great festive spirit happening in every index. Houses are decorated, people resort to shopping sprees, lanterns i..e "kandils" of all shapes and sizes are bought and hung at the windows, "rangolis" are drawn and small earthern lamps called "diyas" or "pantis" are lighted at the door. Perhaps the most important aspect about this festival is the bursting of firecrackers for all four days of Diwali. Of course, people also make merry by distributing and consuming sweets and chocolates.

In our society, although there is no official Diwali celebration, every resident enjoys this festival in his own way. The entire society is lit up with lanterns and "pantis" and at the centre of the society, there is a huge lantern put up for display. The bursting of firecrackers takes place in every nook and corner of the society as well as on the terraces. Of course, on the day of "Lakshmi Poojan", there is a small ceremony taking place in the evening in the Tatyasaheb Oak hall which is attended by mostly members of the Lokmanya Bhagini Mandal. In fact, this year, to commemorate the 50th anniversary of the Lokmanya Mandal, a grand ceremony was organised by the members of the Bhagini Mandal and the Lokmanya Mandal on the old badminton court of the society on the occasion of "Tulshicha Lagna" and this event was celebrated in the most traditional manner and with great success in the presence of many residents.
