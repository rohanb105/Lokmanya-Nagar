Ganeshotsav


Come August and all eyes are set on the calendar to mark the date of "Ganesh Chaturthi" or "Ganeshotsav". Also known as Maharashtra's favourite deity, Lord Ganesha's festival is celebrated amidst great pomp and splendour not only amongst Maharashtrians but also amongst other Hindu communities. Beautiful Ganesh idols of different shapes, sizes and colours are brought home by devotees amidst cries of "Ganpati Bappa Morya" and "Mangalmurti Morya". The idol is kept for different number of days by different people depending upon their prevailing family custom and convenience and are usually kept for one and a half, five, seven or ten days. Besides, all over Mumbai, there are ganesh pandals erected for the purpose of "sarvajanik ganpatis". Often, there are prizes kept for the most beautiful and artistic decorations for such mandals. In fact, the idea of celebrating Ganeshotsav was started nearly a century back by the great freedom fighter Lokmanya Tilak whose intention was to unite the Hindus through the medium of such a festival.

In Lokmanya nagar, there are many members who have ganpati idols at their place during the festival. A majority of the members bring the idols for one and a half days. Of course the main attraction is the sarvajanik ganpati of our society which is also kept for one and a half days and the idol is installed in the Tatyasaheb Oak hall on the ground floor of the I building. The festival is organised and celebrated under the auspices of the Lokmanya Mandal. Donations are also asked for by the Mandal to the residents of the society. The idol is brought the earlier night by which time the decoration i.e. "aras" as we call it, is nearly ready. Normally, the decoration is made of thermocol. Earlier, enthusiastic residents, especially youngsters used to get together one month before the festival every night and work upon the decoration. However, nowadays the decoration is bought from outside by members of the Lokmanya Mandal. One of the exciting aspects of the festival in our society is the "artis", especially the main arti and the last arti on the second day, which is chanted by all, young and old with great enthusiasm and fervour. Nowadays, a good practice started in our society is the chanting of the "Ganpati Atharvashirsha" by our residents on one of the days. All four pujas for both the days are performed by residents of our society.

The immersion or "visarjan" is on the second day evening at around 6 p.m. and is done at the Dadar Chowpatty. A hand cart is hired for the evening and around 8-10 idols of interested residents along with that of the society are kept on the cart and taken for immersion amidst shouts and cries of "Ganpati Bappa Morya, Mangalmurti Morya and Pudhchya Varshi Lavkar Ya".
