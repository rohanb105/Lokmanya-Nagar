Gokulashtami


One of the most popular festivals, it is celebrated to signify the birth of Lord Krishna or "Govinda" as we affectionately call him. It is usually celebrated in the month of August, about a fortnight before Ganeshotsav. On the festival day, under the auspices of Lokmanya Mandal, four "dahi handis" are tied up in our society with the help of our office staff in different parts of the society. The height of the handis varies from one storey to two storeys and the main handi which is broken at the end is tied right in the centre of the society. This festival is enjoyed by not only those who come down and take part in the breaking of the handis but also by those residents who watch with great pleasure such breaking. Amidst cries of "we want garam pani", the "toli" of "young" govindas roam throughout the society enjoying the bucketful of hot water poured down upon them by enthusiastic residents from their buildings. One by one, all four handis are eventually broken with the help of pyramids of youngsters and men surrounding them. Once the handi is broken, there is a great rush for consuming the prasad of cucumber, coconut and banana stored in the handis.

Once all the handis are broken, then the entire troupe marches towards the "I" side of the society where on the stairs of the small hall, a delicious offering of "dahi pohe" and coffee is served by volunteers of the Lokmanya Mandal. Normally, on the same morning, contributions are given by the residents of the society in the form of money, curd, butter milk and "pohe". The money collected is utilised to meet the expenses of the festival and the edible contents are skilfully mixed with other ingredients such as cucumbers, nuts by the members of the Bhagini mandal to prepare an absolutely outstanding delicacy called "dahi pohe"

And to top it all, some of the money collected is later used to buy and consume piping hot "bhajias" to the sheer excitement and enjoyment of the "govindas".
