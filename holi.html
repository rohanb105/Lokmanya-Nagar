Holi


This colourful of all festivals is usually celebrated in the month of March. It consists of two celebrations viz. on the actual "Holi" day, at night, a huge bonfire is lit up just at the entrance of the society and residents offer their "prasad" in the form of "coconuts" in the bonfire, which is of course taken out after some time with the help of a stick and distributed and eaten. According to the Hindu mythology, at this auspicious time, one is expected to throw in the holy fire all the sins that may be committed throughout the year and vow to start life afresh on a good and noble note.

However, in the society, the real attraction of Holi for so many years has been that of "Dhulivandan" or "Rangapanchami" which is celebrated the next day. The whole atmosphere is vibrant and full of joy and enthusiasm since morning. Once can see small kids and youngsters soaking wet and fully drenched in a variety of colours, paint etc. The idea is to spray colours, water with the help of pistons of different shapes and sizes on anyone that you encounter on the way!! Of course, balloon throwing is an exciting part of this celebration, something that the kids and youngsters engage in with tremendous zeal and fervour. By afternoon, everyone is hungry and usually a grand feast in the form of "Puranpoli" awaits them at their respective homes!
