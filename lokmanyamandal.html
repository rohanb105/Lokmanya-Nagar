Lokmanya Mandal


The Lokmanya Mandal, as an institution was first established in 1953. The principle aims of establishing this institution were solving problems on day to day basis in respect of collective and co-operative life in the society and to promote and encourage cultural, religious and social events in the society. One of the important objectives of this institution was also to encourage those people to become its members, who otherwise were not eligible due to the fact that they were not members of the society i.e. they did not own flats in the society. Naturally, the younger generation amongst these people was tapped and inspired to train themselves for a co-operative and collective atmosphere in the society.

The Lokmanya Mandal celebrated its silver jubilee in 1978 at which time the chief guest was the then famous playwright and director, Mr. Purshottam Darvhekar. Not only was the famous play "TO MI NAVECH" enacted by a professional institute called "Natya Sampada" that year in our colony, but a Souvenir magazine was also published and released to commemorate the silver jubilee year of the Lokmanya Mandal.

This year, the Lokmanya Mandal is 59 years old and has come a long way in establishing its aims and objectives. Looking back over the past 59 years, the Lokmanya Mandal has really been a symbol displaying deep sense of camaraderie, social responsibility and community living amongst its members for so many years. Today, the Lokmanya Mandal is chiefly responsible for organising the Ganeshotsav, Kojagiri programme, Gokulashtami, Dassera as also its own separate day amongst the Society Day events. Of course, besides the above yearly events organised by the Mandal, there have been numerous occasions in the past where the Mandal has organised programmes such as children's cartoon and drawing workshops, inviting illustrious speakers from various fields of life, one week yoga camp, annual hikes and picnics, a one hour fun time at the Mahatma Gandhi swimming pool etc. Further, the Lokmanya Mandal also sends its two (minimum) representatives on the Society Day committee every year.
