Managing Committee


In a co-operative housing society, and that too in a society as big as ours, the Managing Committee is perhaps the most powerful and responsible organisation. Powerful, because under the Co-operative Societies Act and the bye-laws, the Managing Committee of a society is given wide powers to ensure the smooth functioning of the society. Responsible, because it has to perform several duties and responsibilities on a daily/regular basis. The Managing Committee consists of elected members and is headed by a Chairman. It also consists of a Treasurer, Secretary etc. The person at the helm of affairs is the President of the society. The first managing committee of our society was formed in 1946 at the time of its inception. It is pertinent to note that only a member of the society can be elected as a member of the Managing Committee. The Managing Committee plays both, a supervisory role as well as an administrative role. It is also responsible for calling and holding meetings such as AGMs and EGMs as and when the need arises. It is generally involved in catering to the needs and demands of the residents and members of the society.

A good precedent set out in our society is that of cooperation and democracy. The Managing Committee members are usually selected by consensus. If any elections are necessitated, such elections, they are free and fair and there is adequate freedom of speech and expression to all members alike. Major issues are decided only at General Body by Consensus rather than by vote. Although there have been minor incidents of controversy and dispute in the past in our society at general meetings, at all times, good sense, peace and harmony have prevailed due to mutual respect the members hold for each other.
